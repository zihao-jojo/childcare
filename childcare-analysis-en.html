03 / Structural Barriers

Why "One App" Is
Structurally Blocked

CCS Government Certification

Software must be registered with Dept of Education and tested against CCSS. Significant barrier to entry.

High Switching Costs

Years of child records, billing history, CCS integration. Centres won't switch unless forced.

APIs Won't Open

Incumbents have zero incentive to let an aggregator commoditize them. No regulation forcing interoperability.

PE-Backed Consolidation

Xplor acquired QikKids, MyWaitlist, Discover. Procare sold for US$1.75B. Consolidation via M&A, not product.

So instead of fighting incumbents, what problem CAN we solve?

05 / Opportunity A — Waitlists

The Waitlist
Paradox

~80%
National average occupancy

20% of spots sit empty on any given day — yet parents wait 12-24 months. This isn't a supply problem. It's an information asymmetry problem.

The Phantom Waitlist Effect

Parents register at 5-10 centres. A centre sees 50 names but real demand is ~5 families. They can't plan staffing or room openings. Parents can't gauge their real chances.

Wait Times by City

Sydney: 12-24mo · Melbourne: 10-18mo · Perth: 18mo+ · Infant (0-2): worst of all
